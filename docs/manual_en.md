### VS CPU

## Game Overview

Card Slot is a two-player competitive card game inspired by slot machine symbols.

### Basic Rules
- Place cards on a 3×3 board
- Form lines with three matching symbol cards
- When a line is completed, its card effect activates
- Win through strategic placement and hand management

### Game Objective
In this game, **survival comes first**. If you run out of cards in your hand, you lose immediately. Use card effects wisely to maintain your hand while creating advantageous situations against your opponent.

## Card Types

The deck contains **52 cards** total, with 6 different symbols.

| Symbol | Count | Effect | Score |
|--------|-------|--------|-------|
| **Rainbow 7** | 5 | Instant win with 3 in a line | -1 |
| **Silver 3** | 5 | Immediately ends the game | -1 |
| **Cherry** | 8 | Pick 1 card from the board | +1 |
| **Watermelon** | 8 | Draw 2 cards from deck | +2 |
| **Bell** | 13 | Draw 1 card from deck | +1 |
| **REPLAY** | 13 | Automatically place 1 card | 0 |

### Card Characteristics

#### Rainbow 7 - Rare Card
- **Ultimate Victory**: Three in a line results in instant win
- **High Risk**: Negative points if left in hand
- **Usage**: Save as your trump card for decisive moments

#### Silver 3 - Game Ender
- **Forced End**: Three in a line immediately empties the deck and ends the game
- **Strategic Card**: Use when your hand score is higher
- **Caution**: Dangerous when your score is lower

#### Cherry - Recovery Card
- **Board Control**: Return 1 card from the board to your hand
- **Tactical Use**: Disrupt opponent's plans or secure valuable cards
- **Restriction**: Cannot take cards from the Center Slot (9)

#### Watermelon - Heavy Draw
- **Hand Refill**: Draw 2 cards, a powerful effect
- **Long Game**: Increase your options with more cards
- **Point Source**: High score value makes it excellent

#### Bell - Standard Draw
- **Most Common**: 13 cards make it easy to form lines
- **Stability**: Draw 1 card to maintain your hand
- **Balanced**: Safe with +1 score

#### REPLAY - Auto Placement
- **Chain Action**: Automatically places a card on the board
- **Lowest Number First**: Places on the lowest-numbered empty slot
- **No Effect**: The placed card's line effect doesn't activate
- **Score 0**: Safe to keep in hand

## Board and Lines

### Board Layout

The board is a 3×3 grid with 9 slots, each numbered:

```
+---+---+---+
| 1 | 2 | 3 |
+---+---+---+
| 8 | 9 | 4 |
+---+---+---+
| 7 | 6 | 5 |
+---+---+---+
```

### Lines (8 Total)

A line is completed when three cards with the same symbol are aligned:

#### Horizontal Lines (3)
- Top row: 1 - 2 - 3
- Middle row: 8 - 9 - 4
- Bottom row: 7 - 6 - 5

#### Vertical Lines (3)
- Left column: 1 - 8 - 7
- Center column: 2 - 9 - 6
- Right column: 3 - 4 - 5

#### Diagonal Lines (2)
- Descending: 1 - 9 - 5
- Ascending: 3 - 9 - 7

### Important Slots

#### Center Slot (9)
- **Most Critical Position**: Involved in 4 different lines
- **Special Rule**: Cannot be directly taken by card effects
- **First Turn Restriction**: Rainbow 7 and Silver 3 cannot be placed here initially

#### Slots 3 and 7
- **Forced Refresh Targets**: Automatically replaced when board is full
- **Strategic Value**: Useful for temporary placements due to high turnover

## Game Setup

1. Shuffle all 52 cards to create the deck
2. Deal **10 cards** to each player (this is your starting hand)
3. Place remaining cards face-down as the deck
4. Prepare a discard pile area
5. Determine first player (rock-paper-scissors, etc.)

### Miracle Hand - Ultra-Rare Instant Win

If your starting hand consists of:
- 5 Rainbow 7 cards
- 5 Silver 3 cards

You can declare "Miracle Hand" and win immediately! (This is astronomically unlikely)

## How to Play

### First Turn (First Player Only)

The first player follows special rules:

1. Choose 1 card from your hand
2. **Must place on Center Slot (9)**
3. **Cannot place Rainbow 7 or Silver 3**
4. Turn ends

### Normal Turn

At the start of your turn, **if the board is completely full**:
- "Forced Refresh" automatically activates
- The card in Slot 3 is discarded and replaced with a new card from the deck
- The card in Slot 7 is discarded and replaced with a new card from the deck
- Line effects do not activate during this process

Then, proceed with the following steps:

#### 1. Choose a Card
Select 1 card from your hand.

#### 2. Place the Card
- Place it on any empty slot
- If no slots are empty: First discard 1 card from the board (except Center Slot 9), then place your card

#### 3. Resolve Line Effect
- If one or more lines are completed, **you must choose exactly 1 line** to resolve
- Even if multiple lines are completed, **resolve only 1 line**
- You must resolve even if the effect is disadvantageous

#### 4. End Turn
Your opponent's turn begins.

## Victory Conditions

### Immediate Defeat Condition
**Running out of cards in your hand results in immediate defeat.** This is the most critical rule.

### Victory Patterns

#### 1. Rainbow 7 Instant Win
- Three Rainbow 7 cards in one line results in **immediate victory**
- You win even if it was your last card

#### 2. Opponent Defeat
- You win when your opponent's hand becomes empty

#### 3. Score Battle When Deck Runs Out
When the deck reaches 0 cards, the game ends and **hand score** determines the winner:

##### Score Calculation

| Card | Score |
|------|-------|
| Rainbow 7 | -1 |
| Silver 3 | -1 |
| Cherry | +1 |
| Watermelon | +2 |
| Bell | +1 |
| REPLAY | 0 |

- **Higher score wins**
- Ties result in a draw
- Players with 0 cards have already lost and cannot win

## Card Effect Details

When a line is completed, that card's effect activates.

### Rainbow 7 Effect
**Instant victory!**

### Silver 3 Effect
1. Discard the line cards
2. **Immediately set the deck to 0**
3. Game ends and score battle begins

**Strategic Points**:
- Use when your score is higher
- Effective when opponent has few cards
- Dangerous when your score is lower

### Cherry Effect
1. Discard the line cards
2. **Choose 1 card from the board and add it to your hand**
3. If no valid cards exist, this effect is skipped

**Restrictions**:
- Cannot take cards from Center Slot (9)

**Strategic Points**:
- Recover opponent's valuable cards
- Secure your own Rainbow 7 from the board
- Disrupt line formations

### Watermelon Effect
1. Discard the line cards
2. **Draw 2 cards from the top of the deck and add them to your hand**

**Special Notes**:
- If only 1 card remains in deck, draw only that card
- The deck then becomes 0 and the game ends
- You must accept drawn cards even if disadvantageous

**Strategic Points**:
- Increase hand size for more options
- Excellent as a score source
- Very powerful in long games

### Bell Effect
1. Discard the line cards
2. **Draw 1 card from the top of the deck and add it to your hand**

**Strategic Points**:
- Basic method to maintain hand size
- Easiest to form lines with (13 cards)
- Enables stable hand management

### REPLAY Effect
1. Discard the line cards
2. Execute **Replay Action**

#### What is Replay Action?
1. Draw 1 card from the top of the deck
2. Place it on the **lowest-numbered empty slot**
3. Line effects do not activate from this placement
4. Replay Actions do not chain

**Strategic Points**:
- Fills the board
- Placement location is predictable
- Advances the board without spending hand cards

## Strategy Tips

### Basic Strategy: Survival First
**"Survive First, Win Later"** - Stay alive first, win second.

- Keep your hand from getting too low
- Refill with Bell or Watermelon
- Save Rainbow 7 as your trump card

### Importance of Center Slot (9)
- Most critical position, involved in 4 lines
- Cannot be taken by card effects, making it stable once placed
- Use for placing important cards

### Utilizing Slots 3 and 7
- Subject to Forced Refresh replacement
- Use for short-term placements
- Don't place cards you want to keep long-term

### When to Use Silver 3
- When your hand score is high
- When opponent has few cards
- When you want to eliminate Rainbow 7 from your hand

### Line Selection Tactics
- Choose carefully when multiple lines complete
- Predict the next turn's board state
- Avoid creating advantageous lines for opponent

### Hand Management
- Rainbow 7 and Silver 3 are negative points
- But Rainbow 7 offers instant win potential
- Balance score and tactics

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1. What happens if my hand becomes empty?
**A.** You lose immediately. This is an absolute rule.

### Q2. If multiple lines complete, do I resolve all of them?
**A.** No, you **choose and resolve only 1 line**. Unselected lines remain on the board (however, other lines may break if the resolved line's cards are discarded).

### Q3. Must I resolve a line even if the effect is bad for me?
**A.** Yes, line resolution is **mandatory** once completed.

### Q4. Can I take the Center Slot (9) card with Cherry?
**A.** No, you **cannot take** cards from the Center Slot.

### Q5. What happens if Center Slot (9) becomes empty?
**A.** Once empty, it becomes a normal slot, and Rainbow 7 or Silver 3 can be placed there.

### Q6. What happens when the deck reaches 0?
**A.** The game ends immediately. Determine winner by hand score.

### Q7. What if the deck runs out during Forced Refresh?
**A.** The game ends immediately when the deck reaches 0.

### Q8. If a line completes from REPLAY placement, does it activate?
**A.** No, line effects do not activate during Replay Action.

### Q9. If I win with Rainbow 7 but my hand becomes empty?
**A.** Rainbow 7 instant win takes priority. You win.

### Q10. What's the probability of Miracle Hand?
**A.** Astronomically low, but it exists within the rules.
